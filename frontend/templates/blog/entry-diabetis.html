Loose 4 pounds a month!

by Hanaa Alraei

30.12.22

Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects the way the body processes blood sugar. It is important for people with diabetes to manage their blood sugar levels through a combination of medication, diet, and exercise. Losing weight can be an effective way to help manage diabetes, as excess weight is a risk factor for the development of the condition and can make it more difficult to control blood sugar levels. Here are a few key points to consider when it comes to losing weight with diabetes: Consult with a healthcare provider: It is important to discuss weight loss goals and plans with a healthcare provider, as they can provide personalized guidance and support. They can also help monitor blood sugar levels and adjust medications as needed. Focus on healthy eating: A healthy diet is an important part of managing diabetes and losing weight. This typically includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein sources, as well as limiting added sugars and saturated fats. Get regular physical activity: Exercise can help improve blood sugar control and support weight loss. It is important to get at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week, or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise. Consider working with a dietitian: A dietitian can provide expert guidance on how to create a healthy eating plan that meets the specific needs of someone with diabetes.
